Tracking Net Worth the Simple Way

3–5 minute read • Posted by FIREandLife

When it comes to financial independence (FI), there are endless debates about withdrawal rates, tax optimization, and portfolio strategy. But beneath all of that is one deceptively simple number: your net worth.

Net Worth = Assets – Liabilities
  • Assets: cash, investments, retirement accounts, HSA, property equity, etc.
  • Liabilities: mortgages, student loans, auto loans, credit cards, etc.

How Often Should You Track It?

  • Monthly → best for momentum and quick course-correction.
  • Quarterly → smooths market noise if month-to-month swings stress you out.

The best cadence is the one you’ll actually stick with.

What Should You Use?

  • Spreadsheet: simple, flexible, private.
  • Apps: automated connections (trade privacy/fees for convenience).
  • Coming soon: a clean, free FIREandLife Net Worth Tracker you can copy. Join the newsletter to get it first.

Make It Motivating, Not Stressful

Some months your net worth dips. That’s normal. What matters is the long-term trend. Each time you update, jot one win:

  • “Paid an extra $100 on student loans.”
  • “Upped 401(k) by 1%.”
  • “Skipped lifestyle creep after a raise.”

The LIFE Side of FIRE

Tracking isn’t about obsessing over a number—it’s about clarity. When you know where you stand, you can make better choices about your time, work, and family. FI is not only about retiring early; it’s about living better now.

Next Steps

  1. Create (or open) a simple sheet with two columns: Assets and Liabilities.
  2. Pick a recurring date you’ll update (1st of the month works great).
  3. Plot the number over time—trend up is the goal, not perfection.
