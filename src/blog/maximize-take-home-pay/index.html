Maximize Your Take-Home Pay

Reading time: ~6 minutes

Increasing your income isn’t only about raises or side hustles—how you manage benefits, contributions, and withholdings can have a huge impact on what actually lands in your checking account. This article walks through simple, practical levers you can adjust today.

1. Optimize Pre-Tax Contributions

Contributions to accounts like your 401(k), HSA, or FSA reduce taxable income. Even a small increase in contributions can lower your tax liability while building long-term wealth.

2. Review Your Withholding

Many employees are withholding too much or too little. Use the IRS withholding calculator to see if you should adjust your W-4 so you’re not giving the IRS an interest-free loan or facing a surprise bill.

3. Leverage Employer Benefits

From commuter benefits to dependent care FSAs, your HR portal may have hidden gems. These can boost your effective take-home pay by covering expenses with pre-tax dollars.

4. Compare Health Plan Options

High-deductible health plans often come with HSA eligibility—combining lower premiums with tax-free savings for healthcare. Depending on your situation, this can free up monthly cash flow.

5. Track the Real Impact

Use our Take-Home Pay Calculator to model different contribution levels, tax scenarios, and benefits. Seeing the numbers side by side makes it easier to choose the contribution mix that balances cash flow and long-term growth.
